The opportunity to receive health care is defined as medical access. In Athens County of Southeast Ohio, many residents struggle to get proper care as a result of the county’s rural landscape and transportation barriers.

EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES

The Athens County EMS station often has difficulty finding homes or accidents in the area. The Chief of the Athens County EMS, Rick Calebs said the ambulance searching for the logging accident on Mountville was challenging to find from the description from the caller.

The ambulance only knew the road and it is a particularly long road, the ambulance did not know which end to enter and had nothing to go on."
Rick Calebs, the chief of Athens County Medical Services (ACMS)

The ambulance was traveling down the road, waiting to be flagged down by the caller. It was unable to see the accident from the road. The Bureau of Workers’ Compensation is investigating this accident to discover if the logging company is an unsafe workplace.

This accident is one of many instances where finding a caller is problematic. Calebs describes a pethura of issues the EMTs have locating people in need.

It’s not as simple as you find in an urban area where they just give you a street address and you can go there."
Rick Calebs

MOBILE HEALTH CLINIC

INFO ABOUT DAVE GOES HERE

Sherri Oliver, the Executive Director for Community Health Programs and Area Health Education Center, helps to run a free clinic on campus through the Heritage College of Osteopathic Medicine as well as two mobile health units that serve 24 counties in Southeast and Eastern Ohio.

We see people who don’t have access to transportation or don’t have access to reliable transportation."
Sherri Oliver, executive director for Community Health Programs and Area Health Education Center

The mobile health units are essentially “doctors’ offices on wheels.” They are 40-foot long trucks with two exam rooms and a waiting room. Nurse practitioners, medical students and registered nurses all travel in the mobile health unit to counties to provide medical care through various health clinics.

Without the help of these nurses and this mobile unit, I without a doubt would be 6 feet under...this truck has saved my life."
Dave, Mobile Clinic patient
