The opportunity to receive health care is defined as medical access. In Athens County of Southeast Ohio, many residents struggle to get proper care as a result of the county’s rural landscape and transportation barriers.

Sherri Oliver, the Executive Director for Community Health Programs and Area Health Education Center, helps to run a free clinic on campus through the Heritage College of Osteopathic Medicine as well as two mobile health units that serve 24 counties in Southeast and Eastern Ohio.

We see people who don’t have access to transportation or don’t have access to reliable transportation."

The mobile health units are essentially “doctors’ offices on wheels.” They are 40-foot long trucks with two exam rooms and a waiting room. Nurse practitioners, medical students and registered nurses all travel in the mobile health unit to counties to provide medical care through various health clinics.

Without the help of these nurses and this mobile unit, I without a doubt would be 6 feet under...this truck has saved my life."

"But sometimes we could take that, you know, medical health unit within maybe two miles of your house and if you don’t have a vehicle to get to us, sometimes we’re still not totally meeting that transportation gap,” Sherri Oliver, the executive director for Community Health Programs and Area Health Education Center said."

In order to meet that gap, Oliver finds herself and her colleagues at the Community Health Program using grants or community donations to give people gas cards in order to help people travel to their health clinics.

While transportation is a huge barrier for those receiving medical care, being uninsured is another issue.

HOCKING ATHENS PERRY COUNTY ACTION (HAPCAP)

In non-emergent cases, Athens County has access to a cheaper mode of transportation through the Hocking Athens Perry Community Action (HAPCAP). HAPCAP has been working to improve the community’s quality of life in the Tri-County area since the early 1960’s.

One of the five divisions of this nonprofit is transportation, including five transit programs.

Jessie Schmitzer, the Athens and Hocking County mobility manager with HAPCAP, plays a key role in the transportation services HAPCAP offers.

“Athens On Demand Transit is a door to door service that services all of Athens county and services anybody for any reason,” Schmitzer said.

This service was originally for senior citizens and individuals with disabilities for $1 one way, but as of Jan. 1, 2020, can now be used by anyone for the cost of $2 one way.

“I'm seeing a lot of people that might have recurring appointments that they can get transportation to sometimes, but not all the time,” Schmitzer said. “A big reason for that is because our transportation programs are really at capacity because there's such a huge need.”

“So we got together with Athens on Demand Transit and Green Cab, and came up with this really cool collaboration. If someone is stranded at the emergency room, then OhioHealth staff can call either Athens On Demand Transit or Green Cab, depending on the time of day, and they could go pick them up at the cost of OhioHealth and then bring them back home,” Schmitzer said.
